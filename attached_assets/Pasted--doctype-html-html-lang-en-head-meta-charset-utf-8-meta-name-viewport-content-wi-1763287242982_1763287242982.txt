      <h2>Quick answer</h2>
      <p>The Toyota Innova (Innova Crysta) remains one of India’s most reliable used MPVs — strong resale value, robust build and long life make it a sensible family buy, provided you check service history, usage, and perform a thorough inspection.</p>

      <h2>Key takeaways</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>Best value: 2016–2020 Innova Crysta (2.4/2.8 diesel) — balance of features and remaining life.</li>
        <li>Check: ownership history, service records and signs of commercial/taxi use over raw odometer numbers.</li>
        <li>Common issues: suspension bush wear, brake judder, injector/turbo repairs on high‑km diesels.</li>
        <li>Life expectancy: many Innovas surpass 300,000 km with regular maintenance.</li>
        <li>Resale: Innova depreciates slowly — high long‑term value makes higher upfront cost worthwhile.</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Which variant should you buy?</h2>
      <p>For families, the 2.4 VX or 2.8 ZX (Crysta) from 2016–2020 provides the best balance of comfort and longevity. Commercial buyers may prioritise earlier gen 2.5 diesels for lower upfront cost but expect higher wear.</p>

      <h2>Pre‑purchase inspection checklist</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>Service history and ownership records — look for consistent dealer records.</li>
        <li>Underbody &amp; frame inspection — check for rust or structural repairs.</li>
        <li>Test drive: listen for gearbox/clutch issues, check for brake judder and suspension clunks.</li>
        <li>Check for signs of taxi/fleet use (seat wear, flooring, registration class, sudden ownership changes).</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Total cost of ownership (TCO) highlights</h2>
      <p>Expect higher running costs than compact cars but reasonable reliability; budget for larger tyres, periodic injector/turbo servicing on diesels and potential clutch or suspension work on high‑km units.</p>
