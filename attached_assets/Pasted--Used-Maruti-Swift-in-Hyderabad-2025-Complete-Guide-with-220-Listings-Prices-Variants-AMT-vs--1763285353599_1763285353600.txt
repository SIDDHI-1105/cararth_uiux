### Real-World Fuel Economy

Petrol Swift delivers 24.8 kmpl (manual) and 25.75 kmpl (AMT) according to ARAI official figures. However, real-world instrumented testing reveals 14 kmpl city / 19 kmpl highway for manual and 12.7 kmpl city / 19.1 kmpl highway for AMT. Hyderabad owners consistently report 18-20 kmpl in mixed city-highway driving, with highway-focused usage achieving 22-22.5 kmpl in favorable conditions.

The discontinued diesel Swift delivered 22.9 kmpl ARAI with real-world 18-21 kmpl, offering 20-30% advantage over petrol. However, availability is limited to 2012-2019 pre-BS6 models with resale value declining 15-20% faster than petrol due to the 10-year diesel ban in Delhi NCR and potential Hyderabad future restrictions.

### Decision Framework

Choose petrol if driving under 50 km/day or 15,000 km/year, primarily city usage with traffic, planning to sell within 5-7 years, wanting better engine refinement and quieter cabin, preferring lower maintenance hassles (₹3,499-₹6,826 vs ₹5,000-₹8,000 diesel), and accepting slightly higher fuel costs.

**Current market reality:** 85% of Hyderabad Swift buyers now choose petrol due to BS6 compliance, better resale prospects, wider availability, improved refinement of the new 3-cylinder engine, and diesel unavailability in 2020+ models. ## Swift AMT vs Manual Transmission

AMT (automated manual transmission) adds ₹50,000-₹80,000 premium over manual for the same year and condition. The 5-speed AMT uses electronically controlled clutch actuation with manual gearbox and automatic shifting, offering creep function for traffic convenience and manual mode availability.

### Real-World Performance

AMT delivers superior fuel efficiency at 25.75 kmpl versus manual's 24.8 kmpl according to ARAI. Real-world city driving shows 12.7 kmpl for AMT versus 14 kmpl for manual, while highway performance remains nearly identical at 19.1 kmpl versus 19 kmpl. Hyderabad owners report 18 kmpl in Bangalore-level traffic and 22-22.5 kmpl on highways.

AMT provides convenience in heavy traffic without clutch pedal fatigue, though 0.5-1 second gear shift lag is noticeable and can be abrupt in stop-go conditions. Manual offers better control and engaging driving but requires more effort in traffic. The trade-off of convenience for 1-2 kmpl loss proves worthwhile for many city commuters.

### Maintenance Cost Comparison

AMT adds ₹2,000-5,000 annually due to AMT-specific clutch pack servicing every 40,000-50,000 km (₹8,000-12,000 replacement if worn with heavy traffic), transmission oil changes, and sensor calibrations. Manual transmission proves simpler with standard clutch replacement every 60,000-80,000 km (₹4,000-6,000 clutch kit) and fewer electronic failure points.

Over 5 years/75,000 km, manual totals ₹32,000 (₹27,000 service + ₹5,000 clutch) while AMT totals ₹42,000 (₹27,000 service + ₹15,000 AMT maintenance), a ₹10,000 difference or ₹2,000 per year. Resale values remain similar as AMT acceptance grows, with 60-70% of the ₹50K-80K premium recovered.
